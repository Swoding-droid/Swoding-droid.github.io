🌷 Level 1 — What Are Flowers?

Flowers are the reproductive structures of flowering plants. They have existed for over 140 million years and have evolved into hundreds of thousands of different species.

Their colors, scents, and shapes help attract pollinators like bees, butterflies, hummingbirds, moths, and bats.

🌹 Level 2 — Flower Anatomy

Most flowers contain four main structures:

  • 🌸 Petals – attract pollinators
  • 🌿 Sepals – protect the flower bud
  • 🌼 Stamens – produce pollen
  • 🌺 Pistils – receive pollen and produce seeds

🌻 Level 3 — Flower Facts

Some flowers bloom for only a single day.

Others can smell like chocolate, lemons, vanilla, or even rotting meat to attract insects.

Young sunflowers naturally follow the sun across the sky, a movement known as heliotropism.

🌺 Level 4 — Famous Flowers

🌺 Poinsettia

A bright holiday flower often linked with Christmas celebrations.

🌻 Sunflower

These cheerful flowers can turn to face the sun as they grow.

🌷 Tulip

One of the world's most popular spring flowers.

🌼 Level 5 — Deep Dive

Flowering plants (called angiosperms) make up around 90% of all living plant species.

Many flowers display ultraviolet patterns that humans cannot see but insects can. These hidden markings help guide pollinators directly to nectar.

Scientists discover brand-new flower species every year, particularly in tropical rainforests and remote mountain ecosystems.

Flowers have shaped ecosystems, agriculture, medicine, and human culture for millions of years.
